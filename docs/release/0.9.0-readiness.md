## Candidate Thesis

If resumed, shiplog 0.9.0 would make review readiness operational:
set up safely, inspect review-loop status, collect receipts, repair gaps,
rerun, compare, and explain share posture before rendering. The 0.8 Evidence
Repair Loop made rough packets improvable; the current candidate turns that
repair loop into a setup-aware operating loop with review-ready packet quality,
Guided Setup / Doctor, and the read-only `shiplog status --latest` cockpit.

The candidate now includes conservative packet-quality surfaces
(`packet_quality`, Packet Readiness, evidence strength, receipt-backed claim
candidates, missing-context prompts, share posture, and quality diffs), the
setup front door (`init --guided`, `doctor --setup`, `sources status`, and
`doctor --setup --json`), review-loop status (`status --latest` and
`status --latest --json`) as the human/agent control surface over setup,
evidence, repair, diff, share blockers, next actions, and receipt refs, and
budgeted GitHub activity harvest as the advanced full-history evidence path.

The candidate does not write performance-review prose, score employees, require
LLMs, mutate provider records, or turn share profiles into dashboards. It gives
the user a defensible scaffold, a safe next action, and a clear view of what
still needs human context.

## Candidate Contents

- `shiplog status --latest` prints a compact read-only cockpit over setup,
  latest run, packet readiness, source state, repair, diff, share posture,
  blockers, next actions, and receipt refs.

  scripts with stable keys, no secrets, no provider probing, no writes, and
  write-labelled next actions.

  missing setup, no runs, repairable runs, repair-in-progress state, share
  blockers, and ready-to-share state.

  plan, repair diff, runs diff, and share explain so it remains a projection of
  existing receipts rather than a third truth source.

  is blocked and does not offer share rendering while share is blocked.

- `packet.md` opens with `Packet Readiness` and renders `Claim Candidates` when
  receipts support them.

## User-Facing Contract

- `shiplog init --guided` writes local-first setup defaults.
- `shiplog doctor --setup` and `shiplog sources status` are read-only,
  no-network setup readiness surfaces.
- `shiplog doctor --setup --json` exposes setup readiness for agents without
  terminal-prose scraping.
- `shiplog status --latest` is the read-only review-loop cockpit over setup,
  latest run, packet readiness, source state, repair, diff, share posture,
  blockers, next actions, and receipts.
- `shiplog status --latest --json` exposes the same review-loop state for
  agents and scripts.
- `shiplog intake --last-6-months --explain` still creates a first packet and
  durable report from an empty directory.
- `shiplog repair plan --latest` and `shiplog journal add --from-repair
  <repair_id>` remain the safe local repair path.
- `shiplog repair diff --latest` shows repair-state movement by stable
  `repair_key`.
- `shiplog runs diff --latest` shows packet-quality movement across reruns.
- `packet.md` can explain readiness and claim candidates without inventing
  impact or final review prose.
- `shiplog share explain manager|public --latest` is read-only and may be used
  before a redaction key exists.
- Manager/public rendering still fails closed without `--redact-key` or
  `SHIPLOG_REDACT_KEY`.
- Setup-blocked repair plans route users to doctor/source-status before
  write-producing repair commands.
